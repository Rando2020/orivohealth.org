# Instructional Quality Standard

## Purpose

A course is production-ready only when it teaches a learner to reason, execute, verify, and communicate. Content volume is not evidence of quality.

## Scoring model

Every lesson and module is scored from 1 to 5 across fifteen dimensions.

1. Accuracy
2. Completeness
3. Practical relevance
4. Difficulty progression
5. Lesson independence
6. Scenario realism
7. Lab executability
8. Answer-guide quality
9. Assessment validity
10. Source quality
11. Accessibility
12. Interview relevance
13. Portfolio value
14. Content duplication
15. Unsupported claims

### Score anchors

- **1, blocked:** materially inaccurate, unsafe, unsupported, inaccessible, or unusable
- **2, major revision:** significant gaps prevent independent learning or reliable evaluation
- **3, usable with limitations:** concept is directionally sound but needs depth, specificity, or better evidence
- **4, production quality:** accurate, useful, sourced, executable, and appropriately challenging
- **5, exemplary:** independently teachable, deeply applied, rigorously validated, and transferable to portfolio and interview evidence

A five is intentionally uncommon. Automated structural scoring cannot award factual perfection. Human review remains accountable for claim-level accuracy and instructional judgment.

## Required lesson elements

Every lesson must contain:

- a measurable objective
- substantive instruction that teaches the objective
- an implementation standard or defensible best practice
- realistic failure patterns
- an applied scenario
- a recommended approach that answers the scenario
- a guided lab
- clear learner evidence
- an evaluation-oriented answer guide
- synthesized takeaways
- registered primary sources
- review metadata and known limitations

## Production thresholds

A course may not be labeled production-complete when:

- any structural registry blocker exists
- a lesson lacks a registered source
- a lesson lacks review metadata
- a lab lacks steps, evidence, or answer criteria
- an assessment contains an invalid correct option
- a final assessment bank has less than 30 percent application or troubleshooting coverage
- required SQL or downloadable lab validation fails
- exact duplicate prompts exist without an explicit approved rationale
- unsupported accreditation, compliance, security, or endorsement claims remain

## Human review responsibilities

Automated review detects structure, repetition, source registration, and coverage patterns. Human reviewers must still evaluate:

- whether the source supports the exact claim
- whether multiple answers are defensible
- whether distractors are plausible
- whether scenarios reflect actual operating decisions
- whether labs can be completed by the intended learner
- whether the course creates false confidence
- whether vendor-specific details have changed

## Review cadence

- Standards and RFC-focused lessons: annual review or when the governing standard changes
- Vendor UI and product lessons: at least every six months
- Security guidance: at least every six months and after major guidance changes
- High-severity learner feedback: triage within five business days
- Broken source or lab reports: triage within two business days
